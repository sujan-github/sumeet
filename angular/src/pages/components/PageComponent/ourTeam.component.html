Our Medical Team

Our team of well-respected physicians and embryologists, with over 100 years of combined experience in reproductive medicine and the treatment of infertility, includes some of the most accomplished and innovative doctors in this area. What’s more, each member of our medical team has been chosen for his or her ability to combine tremendous professional ability with compassion and understanding for each patient.

Desireé McCarthy-Keith, M.D., M.P.H., F.A.C.O.G.

Dr. McCarthy-Keith is board certified in both obstetrics and gynecology and reproductive endocrinology and infertility. She is a fellow of the American Congress of Obstetrics and Gynecology, a member of the American Society for Reproductive Medicine and was honored by Black Health Magazine as one of Atlanta’s most influential African American doctors. She is honored to serve as Adjunct Clinical Assistant Professor of obstetrics and gynecology at Morehouse School of Medicine and was recognized by Who’s Who in Black Atlanta in 2011, 2012, 2014, 2015 and 2016.

Dr. McCarthy-Keith earned her medical degree from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and also a Master of Public Health in maternal and child health from the University of North Carolina. She completed her obstetrics and gynecology residency training at Duke University Medical Center and a fellowship in Reproductive Endocrinology and Infertility at the National Institutes of Health in Bethesda, Maryland. During her fellowship, Dr. McCarthy-Keith’s research focused on the molecular mechanisms of uterine fibroid regulation and reproductive health disparities. She has special interests in male and female infertility, polycystic ovary syndrome, uterine fibroids and in vitro fertilization. She has authored several peer-reviewed publications on reproductive and infertility topics and has presented her research nationally.

Prior to joining Shady Grove Fertility Atlanta, formerly Georgia Reproductive Specialists, Dr. McCarthy-Keith practiced general obstetrics and gynecology in Virginia and South Carolina. While in Maryland, she treated patients with reproductive endocrine and fertility disorders at the National Institutes of Health and Walter Reed Army Medical Center. Dr. McCarthy-Keith was a lieutenant commander in the United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps and held the position of assistant professor of Obstetrics and Gynecology at the Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences.

Dr. McCarthy-Keith is passionate about her vegan way of life. She shares her enthusiasm for plant-based nutrition with everyone she meets and she encourages her patients to incorporate a healthy diet into their fertility lifestyle. A native of North Carolina, Dr. McCarthy-Keith is the proud mother of two energetic sons. She spends her free time in Atlanta visiting the zoo, strolling the botanical gardens and cheering on the Braves.

Anne Brawner Namnoum, M.D., F.A.C.O.G.

Dr. Namnoum is board certified in both obstetrics and gynecology and reproductive endocrinology and infertility. A native of Atlanta, Dr. Namnoum attended The Westminster Schools and received her undergraduate degree from Williams College. She received her medical degree at Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore, and completed her residency in Obstetrics and Gynecology and fellowship in Reproductive Endocrinology and Infertility at Johns Hopkins as well. She directed the IVF program at Johns Hopkins before returning to Atlanta, where she practiced at Emory and Atlanta Center for Reproductive Medicine.

Dr. Namnoum has also pursued her strong interest in Integrative Medicine, which combines the best of conventional medicine with complementary approaches and a “whole person” focus, including attention to nutrition, lifestyle, and stress management. Her practice interests include infertility and in vitro fertilization.

Dr. Namnoum has published research involving genetic aspects of reproductive dysfunction, endometriosis, and other hormonal abnormalities. She has also written articles and textbook chapters on topics such as obesity and ectopic pregnancies, and has given lectures and workshops involving stress and infertility.

She is passionate about the lifestyle aspects of health and wellbeing and loves sharing medical and scientific research that can help women take a more active role in their health care and self-care. She believes that education, advocacy and encouragement are important parts of the physician-patient relationship. Dr. Namnoum’s hobbies include hiking, reading, and spending time with her family.

Mark Perloe, M.D.

Dr. Perloe earned his medical degree from Pennsylvania State University, Hershey Medical Center, and served his residency in obstetrics and gynecology at the University of Wisconsin. Dr. Perloe completed his fellowship in reproductive endocrinology and infertility at the University of Minnesota and is board certified in Obstetrics and Gynecology.

Dr. Perloe has extensive experience treating conditions related to infertility including polycystic ovary syndrome, recurrent pregnancy loss, menstrual disorders, fibroids, male fertility problems and endocrine and other reproductive health problems. His patients have access to the latest in vitro fertilization techniques including fertility preservation, the use of donor eggs, donor sperm, and gestational surrogacy.

Dr. Perloe has served as principal investigator in numerous reproductive health research studies including innovative IVF treatments, ovulation induction, polycystic ovary syndrome as well as investigational medications for the treatment of male erectile dysfunction. He presently serves on the advisory board of the journal Fertility and Sterility. He has published research in medical journals including Human Reproduction, Fertility and Sterility, Obstetrics & Gynecology, Southern Medical Journal, and the International Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology. In 1986, he co-authored Miracle Babies and Other Happy Endings for Couples with Fertility Problems. Dr. Perloe is the creator of IVF.com and is a frequent speaker at community and professional educational seminars. He blogs regularly on Facebook.com/GRSIVF and has created a YouTube PCOS Awareness Channel.

He has served as consultant and a task force member to the Women’s Initiative of the American International Health Association and was active in establishing the first approved endoscopy-training center in the former Soviet Union at the Russian National Institute of Women’s Health. Dr. Perloe is a member of the American Society of Reproductive Medicine, the Georgia GYN/OB Society and has served as an advisory board member for INCIID, PCOSupport, OBGYN.net and the Endometriosis Association.

He is married, has two children and serves on the board of a local theatre. He also enjoys playing with computers, sailing, traveling and cooking.

Michael J. Tucker, Ph.D., F.I. Biol.

Scientific Director
Recognized in his field as one of the world’s top ten scientists in early human reproduction, Dr. Tucker is widely known and respected for his breakthrough achievements in assisted reproductive technology. A full-time clinical embryologist since 1983, he pioneered the first intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI) birth in the United States, the nation’s first birth resulting from a testicular biopsy, the world’s first birth from frozen donor eggs, and the first pregnancy in the world from immature eggs that were matured after thawing in the IVF lab.

Working in the field of reproductive medicine/embryology for more than 30 years, Dr. Tucker is the scientific director of SGF Atlanta, formerly Georgia Reproductive Specialists, and Shady Grove Fertility. His current research interests include oocyte and embryo cryopreservation, in vitro culture environment, embryo growth and selection, and gamete/embryo micromanipulation.

Dr. Tucker is a zoology and physiology graduate of Bedford College, University of London. He earned a doctorate in reproductive physiology from the University of Birmingham in the UK and is a Fellow of the Institute of Biology in England. A frequent speaker internationally and locally, he also serves as an advisor to Creating a Family, an online resource for adoption and infertility. He acts as IVF lab director for the Shady Grove Fertility group of clinics.

Dr. Tucker lives with his wife and family in Atlanta, Georgia. He is an avid motorcyclist, and still competes at a regional level in off road racing and loves to tour the shrinking planet!

Paula C. Morton

Laboratory Director
Paula Morton has nearly 30 years of experience managing in vitro fertilization and andrology laboratories, including 17 years as Lab Director at SGF Atlanta, formerly Georgia Reproductive Specialists. She is a certified Embryo Lab Director by the American Board of Bioanalysis.

Since 1987, Paula has been laboratory director and embryologist at infertility clinics in Raleigh-Durham, NC, Phoenix, AZ, Atlanta and Rockville, MD. She is also an active member of several clinical and professional organizations, including the American Society for Reproductive Medicine (ASRM), the International Working Group on Preimplantation Genetics, Society for Assisted Reproductive Technology (SART) and the Reproductive Laboratory Technology Professional Group.

Paula has authored dozens of publications and scientific abstracts and wrote chapters for three textbooks on reproductive medicine. Her areas of focus include fertility preservation in adult and pediatric oncology patients and the ongoing evolution of pre-implantation genetic testing of embryos.

She received her Bachelor of Science degree in zoology from the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill and completed graduate studies at Arizona State University. While at ASU, Paula served as a Graduate Instructor in the Department of Biology.

Paula lives in Alpharetta with her husband and their six children, two horses and one dog. She enjoys family travel, gardening (except for the weeds), cooking, reading and spending time with their animals.

Cynthia L. Sweitzer

Lab Manager
Cynthia is an experienced embryologist and has been responsible for all aspects of clinical in vitro fertilization lab work, including egg collection, semen and tissue processing, insemination and micromanipulation, as well as embryo care and transfer. She has been an embryologist at SGF Atlanta, formerly Georgia Reproductive Specialists for over 17 years. Her current research interests include cryopreservation and its benefits in third-party reproduction.

Prior to joining SGF Atlanta, formerly GRS, in 2000, Cynthia has been an embryologist at infertility clinics in Pittsburgh, PA, Atlanta and Rockville, MD. She has published numerous papers on subjects ranging from embryo morphology and micromanipulation to ICSI and cryopreservation of human embryos and oocytes.

Cynthia received a Bachelor of Science degree in biology at Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University in Blacksburg, VA.

She lives in Marietta with her husband and two children. Cynthia and her husband are avid runners and cyclists, because she also likes to bake and cook!
